# Kessel Sabacc -- Official Rules

*"Never tell me the odds." -- Well, actually, in Sabacc you should.*

---

## 1. Overview

Kessel Sabacc is a high-stakes card game played in the cantinas, gambling dens,
and smuggler hideouts of the Star Wars galaxy. This particular variant -- made
famous by the back-alley tables of Kessel and popularized across the Outer Rim
-- is a game of nerve, strategy, and just enough luck to make a Jedi nervous.

Two to six players compete across multiple rounds, betting chips and playing
devious Shift Tokens to manipulate the table. Your goal is simple: survive. The
last player with chips remaining claims the pot and earns bragging rights worthy
of the Mos Eisley Cantina.

Each player holds exactly two cards -- one from the sands of the desert, one
from the blood of the battlefield. The closer your hand is to perfect balance
(a difference of zero), the stronger it is. But beware: your opponents have
tricks up their sleeves, and the galaxy is full of scoundrels.

---

## 2. The Deck

The Kessel Sabacc deck contains **44 cards** split across two families:
**Sand** and **Blood**.

| Card Type       | Per Family | Total |
|-----------------|------------|-------|
| Numbered (1--6) | 3 each     | 36    |
| Sylop           | 2          | 4     |
| Impostor        | 2          | 4     |
| **Total**       | **22**     | **44**|

- **Numbered cards** (1 through 6): Straightforward. What you see is what you
  get. Three copies of each value exist per family.
- **Sylops**: The shapeshifters of Sabacc. A Sylop copies the value of the
  other card in your hand -- if both cards are Sylops, congratulations, you have
  achieved perfection.
- **Impostors**: These cards have no fixed value. At revelation, you roll two
  dice and choose one result as the Impostor's value. Risky? Yes. Rewarding?
  Sometimes. Very Han Solo.

---

## 3. Setup

Before anyone touches a card, credits must hit the table.

1. **Buy-in**: Each player puts up **50 to 200 credits** (agreed upon before the
   game). This forms the communal **pot** -- the prize everyone is fighting for.

2. **Chip allocation**: Based on the buy-in, each player receives chips from
   their personal reserve:

   | Buy-in (credits) | Chips Received |
   |-------------------|---------------|
   | 50                | 4             |
   | 100               | 5             |
   | 150               | 6             |
   | 200               | 8             |

   Chips are your lifeblood. Lose them all and you are out of the game -- no
   amount of Jedi mind tricks will save you.

3. **Initial deal**: Each player is dealt **2 cards** -- one from the Sand deck,
   one from the Blood deck. You always hold exactly one of each family.

4. **Discard piles**: One Sand card and one Blood card are placed **face-up** to
   start the respective discard piles. These are public knowledge and available
   for drawing.

5. **Shift Tokens**: Each player receives a selection of Shift Tokens (one-use
   abilities) at the start of the game. Use them wisely -- once spent, they are
   gone for good.

---

## 4. Turn Structure

Each round consists of **3 turns**. After the third turn, all hands are revealed
and scored.

On each turn, the active player may:

1. **(Optional)** Play a **Shift Token** -- before doing anything else.
2. **(Required)** Choose one action: **Draw** or **Stand**.

Play proceeds clockwise around the table. After all players have acted, the
next turn begins. After the third turn, it is time for **Revelation**.

Think of each round as a Sabacc match within the larger war. Survive enough
rounds and you take it all.

---

## 5. Actions

### Draw (costs 1 chip)

Drawing lets you improve your hand -- but it will cost you.

- **Pay 1 chip** from your reserve (this chip goes to your invested pile).
- **Choose a source** -- you may draw from any of the four available piles:
  - Sand deck (face-down)
  - Blood deck (face-down)
  - Sand discard pile (face-up -- you know what you are getting)
  - Blood discard pile (face-up)
- **Swap or discard**: After drawing, you must either:
  - **Keep the drawn card** and discard your current card of the same family, or
  - **Discard the drawn card** immediately (a bluff move, or maybe you just had
    bad luck).

You must always maintain exactly one Sand card and one Blood card in your hand.

### Stand (free)

Sometimes the best move is no move at all. Standing costs nothing -- you keep
your current hand and wait for fate to unfold. Obi-Wan would approve.

---

## 6. The 16 Shift Tokens

Shift Tokens are one-use special abilities played **before** your Draw or Stand
action. Each token can only be used once per game. They are the wildcards that
separate a rookie gambler from a seasoned scoundrel.

| Token               | Category   | Effect |
|----------------------|------------|--------|
| **FreeDraw**         | Economy    | Draw a card without paying a chip. The house is buying this round. |
| **Refund**           | Economy    | Recover 2 of your invested chips back to your reserve. |
| **ExtraRefund**      | Economy    | Recover 3 of your invested chips. Even better deal. |
| **GeneralTariff**    | Offense    | All other players must pay 1 chip each. A tax on existing. |
| **TargetTariff**     | Offense    | One target player pays 2 chips. Make it personal. |
| **Embargo**          | Control    | The next player in turn order must Stand -- no drawing allowed. |
| **Markdown**         | Disruption | All Sylops in play are treated as value 0 instead of copying. Shapeshifters, neutralized. |
| **Immunity**         | Defense    | You are immune to all opponent Shift Tokens for this round. Like having a personal shield generator. |
| **GeneralAudit**     | Offense    | Any player who chooses Stand this turn must pay 2 chips. Standing around just got expensive. |
| **TargetAudit**      | Offense    | One target player must pay 3 chips if they Stand. Nowhere to hide. |
| **MajorFraud**       | Disruption | All Impostors in play are fixed at value 6. No dice roll, no choice. |
| **Embezzlement**     | Economy    | Take 1 chip from each opponent. Skimming off the top, Lando style. |
| **CookTheBooks**     | Chaos      | Inverts the hand ranking for this round -- the worst hand becomes the best. Absolute chaos. |
| **Exhaustion**       | Disruption | Target player discards their entire hand and redraws fresh cards. Back to square one. |
| **DirectTransaction**| Disruption | Swap your entire hand with a target player. "I'll take what you're having." |
| **PrimeSabacc**      | Special    | Roll 2 dice -- your hand is elevated to Prime Sabacc rank (between Pure Sabacc and Sylop Sabacc). A gamble within a gamble. |

---

## 7. Revelation

After the third and final turn of a round, all players reveal their hands.
No more bluffing -- the cards speak for themselves.

**Resolving special cards:**

- **Sylop**: Takes the value of the other card in your hand. If both cards are
  Sylops, both have value 0 -- the legendary **Pure Sabacc**.
- **Impostor**: The player rolls **2 dice** and **chooses one** of the two
  results as the Impostor's value. Fortune favors the bold -- or at least the
  lucky.

Once all special cards are resolved, hands are compared using the ranking below.

---

## 8. Hand Rankings

Your hand consists of exactly two cards: one Sand, one Blood. The **absolute
difference** between their values determines your fate.

From strongest to weakest:

| Rank | Hand             | Description | Tiebreaker |
|------|------------------|-------------|------------|
| 1    | **Pure Sabacc**  | Two Sylops (both become 0, difference = 0) | No tie possible -- only one can exist |
| 2    | **Prime Sabacc** | Achieved via the PrimeSabacc Shift Token | Dice value (lower wins) |
| 3    | **Sylop Sabacc** | One Sylop + one numbered card (Sylop copies value, difference = 0) | Lower copied value wins |
| 4    | **Sabacc**       | A pair -- both cards share the same value (difference = 0) | Lower pair value wins (e.g., pair of 1s beats pair of 5s) |
| 5    | **Non-Sabacc**   | Different values -- absolute difference between cards | Lower difference wins (closer to 0 is better) |

**Key insight**: In Sabacc, *lower is better*. Balance is everything. A pair of
1s crushes a pair of 6s. A difference of 1 beats a difference of 4. The Force
favors equilibrium.

---

## 9. Penalties & Elimination

Winning is great. Losing costs you.

After revelation and ranking, penalties are applied:

| Outcome | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| **Winner** | Recovers all invested chips back to reserve. Well played. |
| **Loser (Sabacc hand)** | Loses **1 chip** from reserve. A gentle sting. |
| **Loser (Non-Sabacc hand)** | Loses chips equal to their **hand difference**. A difference of 5? That is going to hurt. |
| **Tie** | All tied players recover their invested chips. Nobody wins, nobody loses -- a temporary truce. |

**Elimination**: If a player's chip reserve hits **0**, they are out. No
buy-backs, no second chances. This is not some Core World casino with a safety
net -- this is the Outer Rim.

The chips you lose to penalties are gone. They do not go to other players or
the pot -- they simply vanish, like a ship jumping to hyperspace.

---

## 10. Winning the Game

Rounds continue until only **one player remains** with chips. That player
claims the entire **pot** -- all the credits everyone bought in with at the
start.

There is no negotiation, no splitting, no mercy. The last scoundrel standing
takes it all.

*"Great, kid. Don't get cocky."*

---

## Quick Reference

```
Round = 3 turns
Turn  = (optional) Shift Token + (required) Draw or Stand
Draw  = pay 1 chip, pick from 4 sources, swap or discard
Stand = free, keep your hand

After 3 turns → Revelation → Scoring → Penalties → Next round
Last player with chips → Wins the pot
```
